Why computer-use agents matter now

From assistants to agents

As industry analysis explains, an agent is defined by its ability to perceive state, take actions via tools, and observe results — not just chat. This shift is driving real adoption across marketing, data analysis, and operations.

Source: adlibrary.com

Cost compounds without memory

Re-running the same computer workflow without persistent context repeatedly burns tokens and time. Persistent context and caching are now widely recognised as key to making agents economical.

Source: harro.com

Mainstream momentum

Google has made computer use a first‑class capability in Gemini, reinforcing that real browser and desktop control is becoming table stakes for serious agents.

Source: blog.google
